# Finalsätze

**Made up of a Hauptsatz + Nebensatz**

## One Subject

### **Use um..zu**

Used when there is one subject, i.e., the subject is the same

{% hint style="info" %}
Main sentence + um + extra information + zu + infinitive

The verb in the nebesatz is in the infinitive and goes to the end

If the verb is separable, **zu** goes between the prefix and the verb:
{% endhint %}

**Example**

**Ich lerne Deutsch.**\
I am learning German.

**Ich möchte in Österreich arbeiten.**\
I want to work in Austria.

The subject is the same:

| Part                | Subject |
| ------------------- | ------- |
| Ich lerne Deutsch   | **ich** |
| Ich möchte arbeiten | **ich** |

So we can say:

**Ich lerne Deutsch, um in Österreich zu arbeiten.**\
I am learning German in order to work in Austria.

**Ich gehe früh ins Bett, um morgen fit zu sein.**\
I go to bed early in order to be fit tomorrow.

**Ich stehe früh auf, um pünktlich anzukommen.**\
I get up early in order to arrive on time.

**Ich nehme Geld mit, um einzukaufen.**\
I take money with me in order to go shopping.

Here we do **not** repeat **ich** in the second part.

## Two Subjects

### **Use damit**

Use **damit** when the subject in the hauptsatz and the subject in the nebensatz are **different**.

{% hint style="info" %}
the verb in the nebensatz goes to the end and is conjugated

With modal verbs, the modal verb goes to the very end
{% endhint %}

**Example**

**Ich helfe dir.**\
I help you.

**Du verstehst die Aufgabe.**\
You understand the task.

The subjects are different:

| Part                     | Subject |
| ------------------------ | ------- |
| Ich helfe dir            | **ich** |
| Du verstehst die Aufgabe | **du**  |

So we must use **damit**:

**Ich helfe dir, damit du die Aufgabe verstehst.**\
I help you so that you understand the task.

**Ich mache das Fenster zu, damit das Baby schlafen kann.**\
I close the window so that the baby can sleep.

**Ich helfe dir, um die Aufgabe zu verstehen.** ❌\
That would mean: I help you so that **I** understand the task.

## Both are possible

If the subject is the same, you can use both **um ... zu** and **damit**, but **um ... zu** is usually shorter and better.

### Same subject

**Ich lerne viel, um die Prüfung zu bestehen.**\
I study a lot in order to pass the exam.

Also possible:

**Ich lerne viel, damit ich die Prüfung bestehe.**\
I study a lot so that I pass the exam.

Both are correct because the subject is the same
