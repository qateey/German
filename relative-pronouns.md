# Relative pronouns

**Relative pronouns** are words that connect two sentences and avoid repeating the same noun.

In English, they are words like:

**who, whom, whose, which, that**

In German, they are usually:

**der, die, das, den, dem, denen, dessen, deren**

We use Relative Pronouns (Relativpronomen) to start Relative Clauses (Relativsatze).

You have to decline them.

The Gender (Masculine, Feminine, Neuter) and Number (Singular or Plural) to relate to the corresponding noun.

The case is determined by its position and functions in the [Relative Clause](https://easy-deutsch.com/german-subordinate-clauses/relativsatz/).

**Der Mann ist nett. Ich helfe dem Mann.**\
\= The man is nice. I help the man.

Combine them:

**Der Mann, dem ich helfe, ist nett.**\
\= The man **whom** I help is nice.

### English examples

**The man is my teacher. The man lives next door.**

The man who lives next door is my teacher.

Here, **who** is the **relative pronoun**. It replaces the repeated words **the man** and connects the two sentences.

**I bought a book. The book was expensive.**

I bought a book that was expensive.

Here, **that** is the relative pronoun.
