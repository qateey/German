# Transitive and intransitive verbs

### Transitive verbs

A **transitive verb** takes a **direct object**.

In German, this usually means an **Akkusativ object**.

**Ich kaufe ein Auto.**\
I buy a car.

So **kaufen** is transitive.

All transitive verbs are accusative

#### Accusative

Ask:

**Wen oder was?**\
\= who or what?

**Wohin**

**= where to**

Use **wohin** when there is **movement towards a place**.

Question:\
**Wohin gehst du?**\
Where are you going?

Answer:\
**Ich gehe in die Schule.**\
I am going to school.

### Intransitive verbs

An **intransitive verb** does **not** take a direct Akkusativ object.

It can stand alone or be followed by a preposition, a place, a time, or a dative object.

{% hint style="info" %}
So an intransitive verb may have:

1. **no object -** Ich schlafe
2. **a dative object -** Ich helfe **dem Mann**.
3. **a prepositional object -** Ich denke **an dich**.
4. **a place/time phrase -** Ich komme **morgen**
{% endhint %}

All dative verbs are intransitive, but not all intransitive verbs are dative

#### Dative verbs

**Wem?**\
\= to whom/From whom?

**Wo?**

\= Where is it/location

Use **wo** when something is **already in a place**.

Question:\
**Wo bist du?**\
Where are you?

Answer:\
**Ich bin in der Schule.**\
I am at school.

### Popular Accusative and Dative Phrases

| German idea                         | Case                                | Typical verb type              |
| ----------------------------------- | ----------------------------------- | ------------------------------ |
| **I put/place something somewhere** | Akkusativ after two-way preposition | setzen, legen, stellen, hängen |
| **Something is already somewhere**  | Dativ after two-way preposition     | sitzen, liegen, stehen, hängen |

### The “action” verbs are usually regular

These are the verbs where **you do something to something/someone**:

| Verb    | Partizip II |
| ------- | ----------- |
| setzen  | gesetzt     |
| legen   | gelegt      |
| stellen | gestellt    |
| hängen  | gehängt     |

### The “position” verbs are often irregular

These describe where something **is already located**:

| Verb   | Partizip II |
| ------ | ----------- |
| sitzen | gesessen    |
| liegen | gelegen     |
| stehen | gestanden   |
| hängen | gehangen    |

| Category                         | Akkusativ / Action verb | Perfekt            | Meaning                                               | Dativ / Position verb | Perfekt               | Meaning                         | Memory rule                                                                 |
| -------------------------------- | ----------------------- | ------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------- | --------------------- | --------------------- | ------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Sitting                          | **setzen**              | **hat gesetzt**    | to put/set someone or oneself into a sitting position | **sitzen**            | **hat gesessen**      | to sit / be sitting             | **setzen** = movement into sitting; **sitzen** = already sitting            |
| Lying                            | **legen**               | **hat gelegt**     | to lay/put something flat                             | **liegen**            | **hat gelegen**       | to lie / be lying               | **legen** = put it down; **liegen** = it is lying there                     |
| Standing                         | **stellen**             | **hat gestellt**   | to place/put something upright                        | **stehen**            | **hat gestanden**     | to stand / be standing          | **stellen** = put upright; **stehen** = already standing                    |
| Hanging                          | **hängen**              | **hat gehängt**    | to hang something somewhere                           | **hängen**            | **hat gehangen**      | to be hanging                   | **gehängt** = you hung it; **gehangen** = it was hanging                    |
| Sticking / inserting             | **stecken**             | **hat gesteckt**   | to put/stick something somewhere                      | **stecken**           | **hat gesteckt**      | to be stuck / be located inside | same form; meaning depends on **wohin?** or **wo?**                         |
| Falling / cutting down           | **fällen**              | **hat gefällt**    | to cut down / fell something                          | **fallen**            | **ist gefallen**      | to fall                         | **fällen** = someone causes it; **fallen** = it falls by itself             |
| Lowering / sinking               | **senken**              | **hat gesenkt**    | to lower something                                    | **sinken**            | **ist gesunken**      | to sink / decrease by itself    | **senken** = someone lowers it; **sinken** = it goes down by itself         |
| Frightening / getting frightened | **erschrecken**         | **hat erschreckt** | to frighten/scare someone                             | **erschrecken**       | **ist erschrocken**   | to get frightened/scared        | **hat mich erschreckt** = scared me; **ich bin erschrocken** = I got scared |
| Opening                          | **öffnen**              | **hat geöffnet**   | to open something                                     | **sich öffnen**       | **hat sich geöffnet** | to open by itself               | **Ich öffne die Tür** vs **Die Tür öffnet sich**                            |



| Verb pair                 | Akkusativ / action example                | English                         | Dativ / position example             | English                              |
| ------------------------- | ----------------------------------------- | ------------------------------- | ------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------ |
| setzen / sitzen           | Ich setze mich **auf den Stuhl**.         | I sit down on the chair.        | Ich sitze **auf dem Stuhl**.         | I am sitting on the chair.           |
| legen / liegen            | Ich lege das Buch **auf den Tisch**.      | I put the book on the table.    | Das Buch liegt **auf dem Tisch**.    | The book is lying on the table.      |
| stellen / stehen          | Ich stelle die Flasche **auf den Tisch**. | I put the bottle on the table.  | Die Flasche steht **auf dem Tisch**. | The bottle is standing on the table. |
| hängen / hängen           | Ich hänge das Bild **an die Wand**.       | I hang the picture on the wall. | Das Bild hängt **an der Wand**.      | The picture is hanging on the wall.  |
| stecken / stecken         | Ich stecke den Schlüssel **ins Schloss**. | I put the key into the lock.    | Der Schlüssel steckt **im Schloss**. | The key is in/stuck in the lock.     |
| fällen / fallen           | Er fällt einen Baum.                      | He cuts down a tree.            | Der Baum fällt.                      | The tree falls.                      |
| senken / sinken           | Die Firma senkt den Preis.                | The company lowers the price.   | Der Preis sinkt.                     | The price decreases.                 |
| erschrecken / erschrecken | Der Lärm erschreckt mich.                 | The noise scares me.            | Ich erschrecke.                      | I get scared.                        |
| öffnen / sich öffnen      | Ich öffne die Tür.                        | I open the door.                | Die Tür öffnet sich.                 | The door opens by itself.            |
